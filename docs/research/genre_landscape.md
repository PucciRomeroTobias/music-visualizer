# Genre Landscape — Bounce and Adjacent Micro-Genres

## Target Genres

The core genres this project aims to map:

- **Bounce** — umbrella term, often ambiguous
- **Bouncy techno** — hard, rolling basslines with a bouncing rhythm
- **Bouncy trance** — trance melodies over bounce-style kicks
- **Donk** — defined by the characteristic "donk" sound (pitched-up kick hit)
- **UK bounce** — broader UK-centric bounce scene
- **Hard bounce** — harder, faster variant
- **Scouse house** — Liverpool-origin bounce, often conflated with donk

## Platform Coverage

### Spotify

- Curated taxonomy of ~6,000 genres — **none of the target genres exist as formal categories**
- Closest matches in Spotify's taxonomy:
  - `hard dance`, `happy hardcore`, `makina`, `hard trance`
- Artists in bounce scenes get tagged with these adjacent genres or nothing at all
- Strategy: search by keyword, discover through playlist co-occurrence

### SoundCloud

- Tags are **user-generated and free-form** — much better niche coverage
- Artists and uploaders tag their own tracks with precise subgenre names
- The bounce/donk scene has strong SoundCloud presence (DJ mixes, bootlegs)
- Limitation: metadata quality varies, many misspellings and inconsistent tags

### Last.fm

- User-generated tags with **excellent coverage** of electronic subgenres
- Listeners apply specific tags: "bouncy techno", "donk", "scouse house"
- Tag co-occurrence reveals genre relationships organically
- Best source for understanding the genre topology

## Key Insight

These micro-genres **do not exist as formal categories** in any major platform's taxonomy.
They must be discovered through:

1. **Keyword search** — find playlists and tracks with genre terms in titles/descriptions
2. **Co-occurrence** — tracks that appear together in playlists share genre affinity
3. **Tag aggregation** — Last.fm tags reveal how listeners actually classify music
4. **Artist overlap** — artists who span multiple subgenres connect the graph

## Boundary Genres

These related genres form the perimeter of the target space. They share artists
and tracks with the core genres but are distinct scenes:

| Genre | Relationship to Bounce |
|---|---|
| Hardstyle | Shares the hard kick aesthetic, different BPM and structure |
| Gabber | Faster, more aggressive — some artist overlap |
| Hard trance | Melodic overlap with bouncy trance |
| Pumping house | Similar energy and BPM range |
| Happy hardcore | Shares the uplifting, high-energy character |
| Makina | Spanish/UK crossover, strong donk connection |

## BPM Range

The target genres cluster around **150-160 BPM**, distinguishing them from:
- House/techno (120-135 BPM)
- Hardstyle (150-160 BPM but different kick pattern)
- Gabber/hardcore (160-200+ BPM)
